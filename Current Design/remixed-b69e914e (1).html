11 — Honest Risks

Why you should not invest

First-time fund manager

A decade of creative capital, but no prior fund track record. You’re betting on the operator, not a spreadsheet.

VC cycles are compressing

The traditional 10-year fund cycle may not apply. Markets move in ~2-year windows now. We might be early, late, or building for a cycle that doesn’t exist yet.

The agentic future is uncharted

Nobody has a playbook for what’s coming. The companies we back may be building for a world that hasn’t fully arrived. That’s the bet.

Traditional startup-building may not work

If AI collapses the cost of building software to near-zero, distribution, taste, and narrative become the moats — which is exactly our thesis, but it’s unproven at fund scale.

Geography thesis is early

The India–SF corridor exists as a pattern. Emergent, Composio, and others validate it. But nobody has packaged it as fund infrastructure before.

If these feel like dealbreakers, I respect that. If they feel like the kind of risk worth taking early — before the thesis is obvious — we should talk.

Imagine Fund is creative capital as infrastructure.
